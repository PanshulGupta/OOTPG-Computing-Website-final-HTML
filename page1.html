AI is already being used to detect diseases, such as cancer, more accurately and in their early stages. Unfortunately, a high proportion of mammograms yield false results, leading to 1 in 2 healthy women being told they have cancer. However, the use of AI had depicted that mammograms will be more accurate, since they were clinically tested, and the average accuracy of the test was 99%, a significantly high accuracy. Availability of consumer wearables/medical devices combined with AI has made surgeries more accurate.

 

In addition to this, Artificial Intelligence mainly applies cognitive technology to unlock, analyze, and predict vast amounts of health data and power diagnosis, consequently, this can unlock clinics and hospitals to perform more technical acts, by providing an improved management on: Diseases; Care plans, etc. The plans that are created by the help of new algorithms, means that companies have to consistently develop new and developed machines, to improve healthcare. Along with this new robots that are being developed, are hypothesized to have to potential to change how hospitals function, and increase Life expectancy of individuals, this proposes various advantages, such as, a longer independent life, etc.

   

The ‘IBM Watson for Health’, is where patients can check, or share, along with saving all important medical symptom, alongside with key details, such as Symptoms, Treatments, etc. Consequently, this has helped various hospitals massively, due to the fact that it is significantly faster to use and load data, when compared to humans, thus acting like a volunteer to conclude an accurate diagnosis, or further treatments. Googles ‘DeepMindHeath’, is where algorithms are built in real-time, through a mixture of Machine Learning, and Neuroscience to diagnose problems, treatments, along with symptoms related to the brain, or psychology.
